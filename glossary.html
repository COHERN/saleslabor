P&L DAILY NOTES GLOSSARY REMINDERS
SALES & PERFORMANCE
SALES ID
AUTO-CALCULATED
Shows how far ahead or behind you are versus your sales plan, as a percent. Negative means you're running below plan. Example: -2.7% means actual sales came in 2.7% below what was projected.
VARIANCE (VAR)
ACTUAL vs PROJECTION
The difference between what actually happened and what was planned. Green means you beat the plan. Red means you fell short.
PROJECTION (PROJ)
PLAN
The sales or cost target your store was given for the period. Also called a budget or plan.
LAST YEAR (LY)
COMPARISON
The number from the same time period one year ago. Used to see if the business is growing or shrinking compared to last year.
YEAR OVER YEAR (YOY)
TREND
How this year's numbers compare to last year's. Positive means the business grew. Negative means it declined.
ACT STP %
SALES TO PERCENT
Any number shown as a percent of total sales. For example, if shrink is 2% STP, that means 2 cents of every dollar in sales was lost to shrink.
WTD / QTD
TIME PERIOD
WTD = Week-to-Date, meaning numbers from the start of this week through today. QTD = Quarter-to-Date, meaning numbers from the start of this quarter through today.
P&L LINES
LINE 00 — SALES
REVENUE
Total money brought in from selling products. This is the starting point of the P&L.
LINE 04 — COGS
COST OF GOODS SOLD
What the store paid for the products it sold. Lower is better — if COGS is high, less money is left over as profit.
LINE 05a/b — MARKDOWNS
PRICE REDUCTIONS
Money lost by reducing prices on items to sell them faster — like clearance or sale pricing. Too many markdowns hurt profitability.
LINE 05C — SHRINK
INVENTORY LOSS
Products that were lost and never sold — due to theft, spoilage, damage, or errors. Every dollar of shrink is a dollar that can't be recovered.
LINE 09 — GROSS PROFIT
PROFIT AFTER PRODUCT COSTS
What's left from sales after subtracting what the products cost. Higher gross profit means more money available to cover expenses.
LINE 19 — LABOR
PAYROLL COST
The total cost of paying employees — wages, benefits, and overtime. Labor is usually one of the biggest expenses in the store.
LINE 40 — EBITDA
BOTTOM LINE GAIN
The store's overall profit after all operating costs are accounted for. This is the number that shows whether the store made or lost money for the period.
DAILY OPERATIONS
PROPER PRODUCTION
FRESH DEPARTMENTS
The percent of planned fresh product that was actually made and put out for sale. Under 90% means the department didn't produce enough — which can directly cause missed sales.
TEMP CHECKS
FOOD SAFETY
Required temperature checks on food cases and storage areas to ensure food safety compliance. Tracked as a percent of total checks completed. Under 90% means checks were missed.
PRE-SUB OOS %
ECOM — OUT OF STOCK
Percent of online order items that were out of stock or had to be substituted before the order was filled. Over 3.7% means too many items weren't available, hurting the customer experience.
ON TIME HAND OFF
ECOM — FULFILLMENT
Percent of online orders that were handed off to the customer on time. Any number under 100% means some customers waited longer than expected.
ON TIME TILLING
ECOM — ORDER COMPLETION
Percent of online orders completed on time. A score of 90% means 10% of customer orders were late. The goal is 100%.
