Sales & Performance
Sales ID
Auto-Calculated
Shows how far ahead or behind you are versus your sales plan, as a percent. Negative means you're running below plan. Example: -2.7% means actual sales came in 2.7% below what was projected.
Variance (VAR)
Actual vs Projection
The difference between what actually happened and what was planned. Green means you beat the plan. Red means you fell short.
Projection (PROJ)
Plan
The sales or cost target your store was given for the period. Also called a budget or plan.
Last Year (LY)
Comparison
The number from the same time period one year ago. Used to see if the business is growing or shrinking compared to last year.
Year Over Year (YOY)
Trend
How this year's numbers compare to last year's. Positive means the business grew. Negative means it declined.
Act STP %
Sales to Percent
What percent of total store sales this line represents. Sales is always 100% — every other metric shows its share of that dollar. Example: if shrink is 3% STP, you're losing 3 cents of every dollar in sales to shrink.
WTD / QTD
Time Period
WTD = Week-to-Date. QTD = Quarter-to-Date. These tell you which time window the numbers cover.
P&L Lines
Line 00 — Sales
Revenue
Total money brought in from selling products. The starting point of the P&L.
Line 04 — COGS
Cost of Goods Sold
What the store paid for the products it sold. Lower is better.
Line 05a/b — Markdowns
Price Reductions
Money lost by reducing prices on items to sell them faster. Too many markdowns hurt profitability.
Line 05C — Shrink
Inventory Loss
Products lost and never sold — theft, spoilage, damage, or errors. Every dollar of shrink is unrecoverable.
Line 09 — Gross Profit
Profit After Product Costs
What's left from sales after subtracting what the products cost. Higher gross profit means more money to cover expenses.
Line 19 — Labor
Payroll Cost
Total cost of paying employees — wages, benefits, and overtime. Usually one of the biggest expenses in the store.
Line 40 — EBITDA
Bottom Line Gain
The store's overall profit after all operating costs. Shows whether the store made or lost money for the period.
Daily Operations
Proper Production
Fresh Departments
The percent of planned fresh product actually made and put out for sale. Under 90% means the department didn't produce enough — which can directly cause missed sales.
Temp Checks
Food Safety
Required temperature checks on food cases and storage areas. Tracked as a percent of total checks completed. Under 90% means checks were missed.
Pre-Sub OOS %
Ecom — Out of Stock
Percent of online order items that were out of stock or substituted before the order was filled. Over 3.7% means too many items weren't available, hurting the customer experience.
On Time Hand Off
Ecom — Fulfillment
Percent of online orders handed off to the customer on time. Any number under 100% means some customers waited longer than expected.
On Time Tilling
Ecom — Order Completion
Percent of orders completed on time. A score of 90% means 10% of customer orders were late. Goal is 100%.
